About Me

After discovering my appreciation of physical geography at A-Levels, I completed a 3 year degree in geology at the university of Birmingham. I learnt scientific methods of analysing tectonic and seismic data, as well as using R language to identify trends in climate change predictions. My experience coding in my course led me to a realisation that I had a far greater vision in a more innovative and modern role, leading me to take the General Assembly Software Engineering Immersive course.

Through this I evolved a great understanding of how combining programming and my creative mindset can lead to better problem solving and developing abilities. I want to explore the possibilities of a career that combines the creativity of application design to the analytical skills I gained during my university degree to take a role that aims to create an accommodating and coherent experience.

As a result, I have emphasised a dedication to a visibly-easing and efficient interface in my work, and I am looking to use my abilities in an ambitious workplace to begin my journey in software engineering. I’d like to take developmental skills I have learnt and apply them to a role with a larger scope of encouraging change towards a workplace of greater efficiency and receptiveness to improvement.

Projects

Museum Mapper

As a group of 4, we created an app for browsing the range of natural history museums and exhibits across England. Together we used Mongo-DB and React to develop our own back-end API, seeded with 30 different museums each with their own details such as image, description, collections, and location for creating an interactive satellite map view.

I created the index ‘Show All’ page which displayed all of the museums in a concise card-view, using API requests and Bulma to style, as well as creating different integrated components for adding reviews and ratings through API ‘post’ requests and displaying them in a clear manner after re-rendering.

Record-ID

For my final project, I decided to go solo to give myself a challenging experience in a time-sensitive scenario. Using a Django/Python back-end and a React front-end, I designed an app for browsing a database of music records, as well as giving the user the ability to add their own to be displayed in the index and on their own profile.

Yu-Gi-Oh TCG Price Index

My partner and I used a free API of the Yu-Gi-Oh TCG database from the internet to display a range of information to create a page for browsing different cards and their individual prices.

This involved developing skills in acquiring data from different objects and data structures from within the API. I personally built the different API requests and the displaying of the information retrieved. We worked together to use application storage and re-formatting API requests to create a ‘wishlist’ system for the user to keep track of specified items.
